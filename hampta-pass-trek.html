About the Trek

Where Valleys Meet
Deserts in the Sky

Hampta Pass (14,100 ft) is one of the most dramatic high-altitude treks in Himachal Pradesh — a trail unlike any other. What makes it truly extraordinary is the sheer landscape contrast you experience over just five days. You set out through dense deodar and oak forests, cross streams fringed with wildflowers, and camp beside glacial meadows that feel almost otherworldly.

As you ascend toward the pass, the world around you transforms. The lush greens of the Kullu Valley give way to barren Himalayan rock, ice bridges, and eventually the moon-like cold desert of Lahaul on the other side — an utterly surreal transition that experienced trekkers call a "parallel universe crossing."

The optional extension to Chandratal Lake (the Crescent Lake at 14,100 ft) adds an ethereal reward at journey's end — its turquoise waters reflecting snow peaks in silence. The trek is graded moderate, making it perfect for first-timers with a reasonable fitness level, guided by Trovia's expert mountain leaders.
